📖 Short Story

A Day in the Life of a Student

Alex is a university student. He wakes up early every day. He likes the morning because it is very quiet. He drinks a big cup of coffee and eats some bread for breakfast. He reads his books before he goes to the campus. He studies computer science, and he really loves learning about new technology.

After his classes, Alex meets his friends in the library. They sit at a large table and do their homework together. Sometimes, the homework is very difficult, but they always help each other. They talk about their lessons and laugh a lot. Alex thinks that studying with friends is very fun and useful.

In the evening, Alex goes back to his house. He is usually very tired, but he feels happy. He cooks a simple dinner, like fried rice or noodles. After dinner, he watches a movie or listens to music to relax. He goes to bed at ten o'clock because he needs a lot of energy for the next day.

🧠 Important Vocabulary

Improve

Improve (Verb)

To make something better than before. If you practice English every day, you will improve.

1. I want to improve my speaking skills.
2. She improves her cooking by watching videos.

Confident

Confident (Adjective)

Feeling sure about yourself and your abilities. You are not afraid to make mistakes.

1. He feels confident when he speaks English.
2. She is a very confident student.

Opportunity

Opportunity (Noun)

A good chance to do something special or important. You should take it when it comes.

1. This job is a great opportunity for me.
2. Going to university is a good opportunity.

Achieve

Achieve (Verb)

To successfully complete something by working hard. It is the result of your good work.

1. You can achieve your goals if you study hard.
2. He achieved a high score on the test.

💡 Grammar Hacks

We use the Present Simple to talk about habits, routines, and things that are always true. It is about your daily life.

For example, "I drink coffee every morning." This means I do it on Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, and so on. It is my normal habit.

Formula: Subject + Verb 1 (add 's' or 'es' for He/She/It).

We use the Present Continuous to talk about things that are happening right now, at this very moment.

For example, "I am drinking coffee right now." This means the coffee is in my hand at this second.

Formula: Subject + am/is/are + Verb-ing.
